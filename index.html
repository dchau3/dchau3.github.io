Kauai Trip July 2022

Kauai Day 1

Our trip started with breakfast at the Oakland Centurion Lounge courtesy of the Amex Platinum card. It was one of the better lounges we enjoyed and a good start to our day.

We arrived Saturday afternoon from our Southwest flight to a beautiful day. The first priority was making it to the rental car location to beat the crowds. We had been advised that the rental car shuttles and lines can take hours on bad days, so Becky went to get our luggage while I made a dash straight to Alamo. I passed by the shuttle line and saw that it was already very long, and with Alamo only being a ~5 minute walk away, I ended up being the first one there. Shortly after a bus full of travelers arrived and I was happy I made the right choice. I had booked one of the compact options for our car rental, but I was fortunate to see that there was one Camaro left and it was part of my rental options! I got in and got to surprise Becky as I rolled up in my temporary new coupe!

It was lunchtime so our first stop was Koloa Fish Market. There were a lot of recommendations for their poke, so we grabbed some of that and a lunch place. It definitely hit the spot after some traveling.

Our trip started at the beautiful Grand Hyatt Kauai Resort & Spa, where we would spend our next 3 nights. We booked our room for 29K Hyatt Points per night:

The resort was amazing from the get-go; seeing the grounds in person was stunning and we quickly unpacked and took advantage by heading out for a swim. One of the highlights of this resort is their man-made saltwater lagoon, and it did not disappoint.

We took it easy on the first day; lounging around the pools and exploring the resort. A short drive away we went to Puka Dogs. We didn't have high expectations but were pleasantly surprised at how much we enjoyed it. Close by was Poipu Beach where we enjoyed the sunset and got to view some of the turtles on the beach.

Kauai Day 2

On the start of our second day, we headed towards Waimea Canyon, starting off with a hike on the Awa'awapuhi Trail. This trail ended up taking us about 3 hours and 30 minutes, with a pretty exhausting uphill climb the whole way back. We weren't as impressed with the hike compared to the other viewpoints, but we enjoyed the workout and met some very nice people along the way.

One of our favorite views of the trip were on the lookouts on the way up Waimea Canyon. It was incredible to see the different texture and colors of the canyon.

On our way back, we stopped by what ended up being our favorite shave ice, Jojo's and a slice of pie at The Right Slice.

We had been pretty tired from our hikes so we kept it easy, enjoying some mexican food at the Taco Libre food truck, taking pictures, and more lounging at the pools.
